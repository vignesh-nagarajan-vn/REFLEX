# REFLEX: Reflexive Equilibrium Fixed-point Learning for Endogenous Financial Markets

A machine learning framework for markets where the data distribution is not fixed but is generated by the model itself. In OTC corporate bond markets, a dealer's quoting policy reshapes future trade flow, spreads, and liquidity вЂ” breaking the standard ML assumption that the data-generating process is independent of the learner.

REFLEX reframes learning as solving for a self-consistent equilibrium: a fixed point where the market dynamics induced by a trading policy are stable under repeated interaction with that same policy.

> **Status note.** The theory and experiments below are a research roadmap, not completed results. The literature review (18 papers, see `references.bib`) is done. The analytic boundary, the multi-dealer extension, and the calibrated phase diagrams are *target deliverables* вЂ” none are proven or measured yet. This README is written to make that distinction explicit so nothing here is mistaken for a finished claim.

## What it does

- **Endogenous Distribution Learning** вЂ” replaces exogenous data `D_{t+1} = P(В·|D_t)` with a policy-dependent system `D_{t+1} = T(D_t, ПЂ_Оё)`.
- **Learned market response operator `T_Оё`** вЂ” a differentiable, trainable operator over stochastic market transitions, replacing hand-built simulators.
- **Fixed-point objective** вЂ” solve `(ПЂ*, D*) = argmin_ПЂ E_D[R(ПЂ)] s.t. D = T(D, ПЂ)`.
- **Stability-aware training** вЂ” penalizes distribution collapse, liquidity fragmentation, and instability under self-induced market adaptation.
- **Implicit liquidity modeling** вЂ” treats liquidity as a latent dynamical field induced by interaction, not an observed variable.

## Core research question

Characterize when the policy/market loop converges to a stable equilibrium vs. fails to converge, as a function of how adversarial the market is.

### 1. Mathematical contributions

The project's novelty claim is that the performativity stability boundary for a structural market model can be derived analytically from microstructure primitives, rather than assumed or empirically swept. This decomposes into five concrete mathematical targets, ordered by priority.

microstructure primitives (GLFT fill-curve curvature + quoting-cost convexity в†’
`Оі`; P&L scale в†’ `ОІ`; toxic-flow feedback slope `|dП„/dh|` в†’ `Оµ`), yielding an

### 2. Data collection and potential sources

Two data needs are distinct and should not be conflated: (a) data to calibrate realistic microstructure parameters (`k`, `A`, `Пѓ`, inventory dynamics), and (b) data/simulation needed to empirically measure `Оµ`, `m`, and the stability boundary across regimes.

**2.1 вЂ” Calibration data (real-world microstructure)**

- **TRACE (Trade Reporting and Compliance Engine)** вЂ” FINRA's mandatory post-trade reporting feed for U.S. corporate bonds; the standard academic source for OTC bond trade-level data (price, size, timestamp, dealer side). Available via WRDS academic access.
- **MarketAxess / Tradeweb post-trade data** вЂ” electronic OTC trading platforms; useful for spread and liquidity metrics on traded bonds, typically licensed/paid access.
- **Bloomberg/Refinitiv terminal data** вЂ” bond static data (duration, DV01, credit rating, issue size) needed to calibrate per-bond inventory risk parameters.
- **FINRA/MSRB academic data programs** вЂ” possible lower-cost or free academic-tier access to trade-level OTC data; needs direct inquiry, as terms change.
- **Federal Reserve / OFR (Office of Financial Research) corporate bond liquidity datasets** вЂ” public aggregate liquidity and spread series useful for sanity-checking calibration, though not trade-level.

**2.2 вЂ” Simulation data (synthetic, for stability measurement)**

- Self-generated via the dealer/market simulator: each run logs `(h, П„)` pairs (quote width, realized toxic flow), inventory paths `q_after`, and induced order-flow distributions `D(П†)` across deployments.
- These logs are the raw input to every estimator in 1.1 (BR-slope, Sinkhorn distance, CKS-implied `dО»_informed/dОґ`) and must be retained across the full sweep of `Оµ`, `N` (dealer count), and bond-universe size for the phase diagrams in 1.3вЂ“1.5.

**2.3 вЂ” Open caveat**

Real trade-level OTC data (TRACE) carries licensing and access lead time; the project should explicitly decide, before experiments begin, whether the headline phase diagrams will be calibrated against real TRACE-derived parameters or run on a fully synthetic, analytically specified microstructure (acceptable for a methodology-first ICAIF/NeurIPS submission, but must be stated plainly rather than implied to be real-market validated).

### 3. Preprocessing

- **Trade data cleaning (if using TRACE or equivalent):** deduplicate reported trades (TRACE reports both sides), filter cancelled/corrected reports, exclude agency trades if isolating principal dealer risk, and align trade timestamps to a common clock across venues.
- **Spread and inventory reconstruction:** derive realized bid-ask half-spreads `h` and inventory paths `q` from sequential trade records per dealer-bond pair; this requires inferring dealer identity and side from TRACE's anonymized-but-attributable fields.
- **Parameter calibration inputs:** compute per-bond volatility `Пѓ`, order-arrival intensity parameters `(A, k)` via maximum-likelihood fit of the exponential-intensity model to inter-trade times, and duration/DV01 from static bond reference data.
- **Simulator log normalization:** standardize logged `(h, П„, q)` tuples across seeds and runs into a consistent schema so that downstream estimators (BR-slope regression, Sinkhorn distance, CKS-implied slope) consume identical input shapes regardless of which sweep produced them.
- **Train/calibration/held-out split for any learned component of `T_Оё`:** split simulation episodes (not individual trades, to avoid leakage across a single equilibrium trajectory) into calibration and held-out stability-verification sets.

### 4. Model architecture

- **Market response operator `T_Оё`:** a differentiable, parametric operator mapping `(D_t, ПЂ_Оё)` to `D_{t+1}` вЂ” i.e., a learned, trainable substitute for a hand-built market simulator. Architecture choice (e.g., neural SDE, recurrent operator, or attention-based sequence model over order-flow state) is an open design decision to be settled against the stochastic-control baselines below.
- **Baseline structural policy:** the closed-form GLFT/AvellanedaвЂ“Stoikov optimal quoting policy, used both as a sanity-check baseline for `T_Оё` and as the source of analytically derived `Оі`, `ОІ` in 1.1.
- **PerfGD correction module:** a component that estimates or consumes the analytic `dD/dП†` (from 1.1) and feeds a corrected gradient into the policy update вЂ” architecturally distinct from blind RRM, which simply retrains on the latest induced distribution.
- **Multi-dealer extension:** `N` instances of the policy/operator pair interacting through a shared induced distribution, required for the systemic-risk experiments in 1.3.
- **Estimator heads (non-trained, diagnostic):** BR-slope regression, Sinkhorn-distance computation (entropic OT), and CKS-implied informed-flow slope вЂ” these run alongside `T_Оё` as measurement instruments, not as part of the trained model.

### 5. Training and tuning

- **Training loop:** repeated risk minimization (RRM) as the baseline loop вЂ” train `ПЂ_Оё` against the current `D_t`, deploy, observe `D_{t+1} = T_Оё(D_t, ПЂ_Оё)`, repeat вЂ” with PerfGD as the corrected variant once 1.1's analytic `dD/dП†` is available.
- **Stability-aware loss terms:** penalties for distribution collapse, liquidity fragmentation, and divergence of the BR-slope modulus `m` away from the contraction regime (`m < 1`), in addition to the base quoting-policy loss.
- **Deploy cadence (greedy vs. lazy):** sweep the number of optimizer steps `K` taken before each redeployment (lazy deploy), since `K` is a stabilization knob вЂ” larger `K` reduces the per-update sensitivity the optimizer sees, raising effective curvature `Оі_eff = Оі в€’ ОµОІ`.
- **Sweep axes for the phase diagram:** feedback gain `Оµ`, number of dealers `N`, and bond-universe size, each swept across multiple seeds to produce median + IQR bands rather than single-run point estimates.
- **Hyperparameter tuning targets:** learning rate and `K` (lazy-deploy steps) for the RRM/PerfGD loop; entropic regularization strength for the Sinkhorn estimator; ambiguity-set radius for the distributionally robust stability point (1.4).
- **Validation criterion:** agreement between the three independent `Оµ` estimators (BR-slope, Sinkhorn, CKS-implied) is the primary check that the trained operator and estimators are measuring the same underlying quantity, not an artifact of one estimation method.
