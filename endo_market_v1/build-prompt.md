## ROLE & MISSION

You are a senior research engineer building a small, rigorous, **runnable** research codebase in Python. The project studies a novel ML problem: **learning where the data distribution is endogenously generated by the model itself.** The concrete domain is an OTC corporate-bond market-making desk, where a dealer's quoting policy changes future order flow, spreads, and liquidity — so the policy and the distribution it learns from are coupled.

The intellectual backbone is **performative prediction** (Perdomo, Zrnic, Mendler-Dünner, Hardt, 2020). Treat each policy θ as inducing a distribution `D(θ)`; we seek a **performatively stable fixed point** where the market induced by a policy is the same market the policy is optimal against. The **headline research contribution** is an empirical characterization of *when the policy↔distribution loop converges vs. diverges, as a function of how adversarial (toxic) the market is.* Everything in the build serves that experiment.

Do not over-engineer. Prefer clarity and correctness over cleverness. The code must run end-to-end on a CPU with small default configs, and scale up via config only.

## FORMAL SETUP (implement faithfully)

- A structural **ground-truth simulator** `T_true` defines the real market dynamics, parameterized by an **adversariality knob `α ∈ [0,1]`** controlling the fraction and signal-strength of informed (toxic) flow.
- A **learned, differentiable Market Response Operator** `T_θ` is a surrogate fit to transitions collected under the current policy. It maps `(market_state, policy_summary) → distribution over next market_state`. It exists so policy optimization can take pathwise gradients through market dynamics.
- A **dealer policy** `π_φ` maps state → quotes (bid/ask half-spreads, and optionally skew) per bond. Provide a **linear** variant and an **MLP** variant behind one interface.
- **Performative risk:** `PR(φ) = E_{s ~ D(φ)}[ R(φ; s) ]` where `R` is the dealer's negative P&L objective plus risk/stability terms.
- **Performatively stable point (the fixed point we solve for):** `φ* = argmin_φ E_{s ~ D(φ*)}[R(φ; s)]`, found via **Repeated Risk Minimization (RRM)**: deploy policy → collect data from `T_true` → refit `T_θ` → re-optimize `π_φ` against `T_θ` → repeat. Track the iterate map `G: φ_k ↦ φ_{k+1}`.
- **Convergence theory hook:** RRM contracts when the distribution map's sensitivity `ε` is small relative to loss smoothness/curvature (`ε < γ/β`). `ε` should rise with `α`. The core experiment estimates the **empirical contraction modulus** `L̂ = ‖φ_{k+1}-φ*‖ / ‖φ_k-φ*‖` as a function of `α`, and locates the critical `α*` where `L̂` crosses 1 (stable → divergent).

## COMPONENT SPECS

### 1. Types (`types.py`)
Define frozen-ish dataclasses (use tensors where differentiability matters):
- `MarketState`: `inventory: Tensor[N]`, `mid: Tensor[N]`, `fundamental: Tensor[N]` (latent true value, hidden from policy), `liquidity: Tensor[N]` (latent field), `flow_recent: Tensor[N]`, `t: int`.
- `Quotes`: `half_spread: Tensor[N]`, `skew: Tensor[N]` (optional inventory-driven shift).
- `Fills`: `qty: Tensor[N]` (signed: + = dealer bought), `price: Tensor[N]`, `was_informed: Tensor[N] bool`.
- `Transition`: `(state, quotes, fills, next_state, pnl_components: dict)`.
- A `policy_summary(state, policy)` helper returning a fixed-length vector describing current quoting behavior (mean half-spread, skew, spread elasticity) — this is the policy-dependence channel fed into `T_θ`.

### 2. Bond universe (`env/bonds.py`)
`BondUniverse(n_bonds, n_sectors, seed)`: generate features (duration, rating ∈ {AAA…B}, issue_size, sector). Build a **cross-bond correlation matrix** (block structure by sector + small global factor) used by the liquidity field. Expose `.features` and `.corr`.

### 3. Latent liquidity field (`env/liquidity_field.py`)
A mean-reverting latent process per bond, coupled across bonds via `BondUniverse.corr`. `step(liquidity, flow, quotes) → next_liquidity`. Tight quotes attract flow that *temporarily* boosts apparent liquidity but raises toxicity; persistent over-tightening degrades liquidity (this is what generates fragility). Liquidity is never directly observed by the policy — only its effects.

### 4. Client flow (`env/clients.py`)
`ClientFlowModel(alpha, ...)`. On each step, sample orders:
- A base **uninformed** Poisson arrival whose volume *decreases* with quoted half-spread (demand elasticity).
- A fraction `α` of arrivals are **informed**: they observe a noisy signal of `fundamental - mid` and trade in the direction the dealer is mispriced (adverse selection). Signal strength scales with `α`.
- Return signed orders + `was_informed` flags. The **policy-dependence** lives here: quotes change which orders arrive and from whom.

### 5. Structural simulator (`env/simulator.py`) — `T_true`
`StructuralSimulator(bonds, clients, cfg)`:
- `.reset(seed) → MarketState`
- `.step(state, quotes) → Transition`: (a) draw client orders given quotes; (b) compute fills at quoted prices; (c) update inventory; (d) evolve `fundamental` (random walk) and `mid` (toward fundamental + impact from flow); (e) evolve liquidity field; (f) compute `pnl_components`: `spread_capture`, `inventory_pnl` (mark-to-market on the fundamental move), `adverse_selection_loss`.
- This is ground truth; it is **not** trained.

### 6. Market Response Operator (`operator/response_operator.py`) — `T_θ`
`MarketResponseOperator(nn.Module)`:
- `forward(state_features, policy_summary) → DistParams` over the next-state delta (inventory change, mid change, flow, liquidity change).
- Output via `heads.py`: default `GaussianHead` (diagonal), optional `MixtureHead` for multimodality.
- `.sample(... , reparam=True)` for pathwise gradients; `.log_prob(target)` for MLE fitting; `.rollout(state, policy, horizon)` to simulate trajectories differentiably.
- Must accept the `policy_summary` as input so the operator is explicitly **policy-conditioned** (the formal `T(D, π)`).

### 7. Dealer policy (`policy/dealer_policy.py`)
`DealerPolicy` ABC with `.quote(state) → Quotes`, `.parameters()`, `.flatten()/.load_flat()` (for iterate-distance computation).
- `LinearPolicy`: half-spread = linear fn of state features (inventory, recent flow, liquidity proxy), passed through softplus to stay positive; skew linear in inventory.
- `MLPPolicy`: same interface, small MLP.
Both consume only **observable** state (no `fundamental`, no true `liquidity`).

### 8. Reward (`objective/reward.py`)
`reward(transition, cfg) → Tensor` and a per-component breakdown. Dealer objective = `spread_capture + inventory_pnl − adverse_selection_loss − inventory_risk_penalty(quadratic in |inventory|)`. We **minimize** `R = −E[objective]`.

### 9. Stability terms & diagnostics (`objective/stability.py`)
Add config-weighted penalties to the policy objective and expose them as standalone diagnostics:
- **Anti-collapse:** entropy floor on the induced flow distribution (penalize degenerate quoting that kills volume).
- **Fragmentation/concentration:** Herfindahl index (HHI) of flow across bonds; penalize pathological concentration.
- **Adversarial-flow concentration:** share of P&L lost to informed flow (toxicity exposure).
- **Deployment instability (the key one):** a **Lipschitz/spectral penalty on the policy→distribution map**, approximated by perturbing `policy_summary` and measuring induced change in `T_θ`'s output distribution (e.g., KL or Wasserstein). This directly regularizes the contraction modulus and is a tunable lever in the convergence study.

### 10. Equilibrium loop (`equilibrium/`)
- `data_collection.collect(policy, T_true, n_episodes, horizon) → List[Transition]`.
- `fit_operator.fit(T_theta, dataset, cfg) → losses`: MLE (`−Σ log_prob`), early stopping, returns held-out NLL.
- `optimize_policy.optimize(policy, T_theta, reward, stability, cfg) → policy, info`: optimize `π_φ` against the **learned** operator using pathwise gradients through `T_theta.rollout`; include a **REINFORCE fallback** path for non-reparameterizable settings. Return updated flat params.
- `rrm_loop.run_rrm(...) → RRMTrajectory`: the coupled outer loop:
  1. collect data from `T_true` under `φ_k`
  2. refit `T_θ`
  3. optimize `φ_{k+1}` against `T_θ` (+ stability)
  4. record `‖φ_{k+1}−φ_k‖`, performative risk on fresh `T_true` rollouts, all diagnostics
  5. stop on convergence tolerance or max iters.
  `RRMTrajectory` stores the full iterate path for analysis.

### 11. Analysis (`analysis/`)
- `metrics.py`: realized spread, fill rate, toxicity (VPIN-style bucketed order-imbalance proxy), inventory variance, HHI.
- `convergence.py`:
  - `fixed_point_residual(traj)`,
  - `empirical_lipschitz(traj)` → `L̂` via successive iterate-distance ratios (robust/median-filtered),
  - `classify_run(traj) → {converged, oscillating, divergent}`.
- `sweep.py`: `run_alpha_sweep(alpha_grid, n_seeds, base_cfg)` → for each `α`, run RRM across seeds, aggregate `L̂`, final performative risk, convergence class. Locate `α*` (where median `L̂` crosses 1).
- `plots.py`: (a) iterate-distance vs RRM step for several `α`; (b) **phase diagram**: `α` on x, `L̂` on y, shaded stable/divergent regions with `α*` marked; (c) performative risk at fixed point vs `α`; (d) toxicity & HHI vs `α`.
